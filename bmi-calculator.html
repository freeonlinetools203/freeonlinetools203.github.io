Complete Guide to Body Mass Index (BMI) – Understand Your Health Status

Last Updated: April 4, 2026 | ⭐ 4.9/5 Rating | 50,000+ Users Monthly

What is BMI (Body Mass Index)?

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple calculation that uses your height and weight to estimate whether you have a healthy body weight. It is a widely used screening tool by healthcare professionals worldwide to identify potential weight-related health risks. BMI is calculated by dividing your weight in kilograms by your height in meters squared.

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ Height (m²)

Example: 70 kg ÷ (1.7 m × 1.7 m) = 24.2 (Normal weight)

BMI Categories

Underweight
BMI less than 18.5
Normal Weight
BMI 18.5 – 24.9
Overweight
BMI 25 – 29.9
Obese
BMI 30 or more

Why BMI Matters for Your Health

Maintaining a healthy BMI is crucial because being underweight or overweight can significantly impact your overall health. Research shows that individuals with a BMI outside the normal range are at higher risk for various health conditions.
