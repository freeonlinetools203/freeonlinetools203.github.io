What is Zakat and Why It Matters?

Zakat is one of the five pillars of Islam and an obligatory form of charity for every eligible Muslim. It means "purification" and "growth" – purifying your wealth and blessing what remains. Every Muslim who possesses wealth above the Nisab threshold must pay 2.5% of their surplus assets to those in need. Our Zakat calculator helps you determine your exact Zakat obligation quickly and accurately. Whether you need a zakat calculator pakistan, zakat calculator india, zakat calculator usa, or zakat calculator uae, our tool supports all currencies.

Zakat = 2.5% × Net Wealth

Net Wealth = Total Assets − Total Debts

What is Nisab and Why It Matters?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes obligatory. There are two Nisab thresholds based on Islamic tradition:

  • Gold Nisab: Approximately 87.48 grams of gold – use zakat on gold calculator
  • Silver Nisab: Approximately 612.36 grams of silver – use zakat on silver calculator

Most scholars recommend using the silver Nisab as it benefits more people. If your net wealth equals or exceeds the Nisab value, Zakat is due at 2.5%. Our zakat nisab calculator automatically checks your eligibility.

Who Should Receive Zakat?

  • 🔹 Al-Fuqara (The Poor): Those who have little to no wealth
  • 🔹 Al-Masakin (The Needy): Those in severe poverty
  • 🔹 Amil (Zakat Collectors): Those appointed to collect Zakat
  • 🔹 Muallaf (New Muslims): Those whose hearts need reconciliation
  • 🔹 Ar-Riqab (Slaves): To free slaves (historically)
  • 🔹 Al-Gharimin (Debtors): Those burdened by debt
  • 🔹 Fi Sabilillah (In Allah's Cause): For the cause of Islam
  • 🔹 Ibn as-Sabil (Travelers): Stranded travelers in need

❓ Frequently Asked Questions About Zakat

📌 What is Zakat?

Zakat is an obligatory form of charity in Islam, one of the five pillars. Every eligible Muslim must pay 2.5% of their surplus wealth to those in need.

📌 What is Nisab in Zakat?

Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth a Muslim must possess before Zakat becomes due. It is based on the value of gold (87.48g) or silver (612.36g). Our zakat nisab calculator helps determine eligibility.

📌 Is this Zakat Calculator free?

Yes, this Zakat calculator is completely free with unlimited use. No registration or payment required. It's the best online zakat calculator available.

📌 Do I pay Zakat on gold jewelry?

Yes, Zakat is due on gold and silver jewelry if it reaches the Nisab threshold, regardless of whether it's for personal use or investment. Use our zakat on gold calculator for accurate calculation.

📌 Can I pay Zakat during Ramadan?

Yes, you can pay Zakat at any time of the year. However, many Muslims prefer to pay during Ramadan due to the increased rewards of good deeds. Our zakat calculator ramadan tool helps during this blessed month.

📌 Does this calculator store my financial data?

No, all calculations happen locally in your browser. We do not store, track, or share any financial or personal information. It's a secure islamic zakat tool.

📌 What is the Zakat rate?

The Zakat rate is 2.5% (or 1/40) of your net wealth that has been in your possession for one lunar year. Our zakat 2.5 percent calculator does this automatically.

📌 Who is eligible to receive Zakat?

Zakat can be given to eight categories: the poor, the needy, Zakat collectors, new Muslims, freeing slaves, debtors, for Allah's cause, and stranded travelers.

📌 How to calculate zakat on salary?

For salaried individuals, add your savings from salary that have been in your possession for one lunar year, then pay 2.5% on the total if it exceeds Nisab. Our zakat on salary calculator makes this easy.
