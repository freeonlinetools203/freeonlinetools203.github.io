🧮 4. Manual Age Calculation Formula (Step by Step)

While our age calculator from date of birth does all the work automatically, it's good to know the manual method. Here's the exact formula to calculate chronological age manually:

Formula: Current Date – Birth Date = Age (with adjustments for months and days)

Step-by-step:
1. Subtract birth year from current year → gives approximate years.
2. Subtract birth month from current month. If negative, subtract 1 year and add 12 to months.
3. Subtract birth day from current day. If negative, subtract 1 month and add days of previous month.

Example: Today is June 14, 2026. Date of birth is March 20, 1990.
Years: 2026 – 1990 = 36 years.
Months: June (6) – March (3) = 3 months.
Days: 14 – 20 = -6 → borrow 1 month (30 days) → 14+30 = 44, minus 20 = 24 days. Because we borrowed 1 month, months become 2.
Final: 36 years, 2 months, 24 days.

Manual calculation is prone to mistakes – especially with leap years. Our online age calculator by date of birth with time avoids all errors and gives you results down to the second.

📆 5. How Leap Years Affect Your Age Calculation

A leap year occurs every 4 years (except centuries not divisible by 400), adding an extra day (February 29). If you were born on February 29, your exact age in days is tricky to calculate manually. Our age calculator for leap year babies automatically handles this: it knows that February has 29 days in leap years and 28 in non-leap years. For example, a person born on February 29, 2000, will have their age displayed accurately – the tool knows how many actual days have passed since that date. Even if you weren't born on Feb 29, leap years affect the total number of days you've lived. Our exact age calculator with leap year adjustment ensures your age in days is always precise.
