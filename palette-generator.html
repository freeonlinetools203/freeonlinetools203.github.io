📊 Types of Color Palettes

  • 🎨 Analogous Colors: Colors that sit next to each other on the color wheel. Creates harmonious and pleasing designs. Perfect for nature-inspired themes.
  • 🔄 Complementary Colors: Colors opposite each other on the color wheel. Creates high contrast and vibrant looks. Ideal for making elements stand out.
  • 🔺 Triadic Colors: Three colors evenly spaced on the color wheel. Creates balanced and rich color schemes. Great for bold, dynamic designs.
  • ⚫ Monochromatic Colors: Different shades, tints, and tones of a single color. Creates clean, elegant, and professional looks. Perfect for minimalist designs.

🎨 Understanding Color Harmonies

Color harmony is the arrangement of colors in a way that is pleasing to the eye. Our color palette generator uses these proven harmony rules:

Analogous Color Scheme

Analogous colors are groups of three colors that are next to each other on the color wheel. They usually match well and create serene and comfortable designs. Example: Blue, Blue-Green, and Green.

Complementary Color Scheme

Complementary colors are pairs of colors that are opposite each other on the color wheel. They create high contrast and high impact. Example: Red and Green, Blue and Orange, Yellow and Purple.

Triadic Color Scheme

A triadic color scheme uses three colors that are evenly spaced around the color wheel. It offers high contrast while retaining harmony. Example: Red, Yellow, and Blue.

Monochromatic Color Scheme

A monochromatic color scheme uses different shades, tints, and tones of a single color. It creates a cohesive and elegant look that is easy on the eyes.
