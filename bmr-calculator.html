🔥 BMR CALCULATOR - Complete Guide

What is Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR)?

BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns while at complete rest to maintain basic functions like breathing, circulation, controlling body temperature, and cell growth. BMR accounts for 60-75% of your total daily calorie burn!

How BMR is Calculated (Mifflin-St Jeor Formula)

  • Men: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) + 5
  • Women: BMR = (10 × weight in kg) + (6.25 × height in cm) - (5 × age) - 161

Activity Levels (TDEE - Total Daily Energy Expenditure)

  • 🛋️ Sedentary (little/no exercise): BMR × 1.2
  • 🚶 Light Activity (1-3 days/week): BMR × 1.375
  • 🏃 Moderate Activity (3-5 days/week): BMR × 1.55
  • 💪 Heavy Activity (6-7 days/week): BMR × 1.725
  • 🏆 Athlete (twice daily training): BMR × 1.9

Why BMR Matters for Your Health

Knowing your BMR helps you set accurate weight loss goals, plan muscle gain strategies, maintain healthy weight, and create effective meal plans.

What is BMR and why is it important? ▼
BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) is the number of calories your body burns at rest. It's important because it represents the minimum energy needed for vital functions like breathing, circulation, and cell production. Knowing your BMR helps you determine your daily calorie needs for weight loss, maintenance, or muscle gain.
How accurate is this BMR calculator? ▼
This calculator uses the Mifflin-St Jeor formula, which is considered the most accurate BMR formula by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics. It provides results within ±10% of actual BMR for most individuals.
What's the difference between BMR and TDEE? ▼
BMR is calories burned at complete rest. TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) includes BMR plus calories burned through physical activity. TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor. Use TDEE to plan your actual daily calorie intake.

Which BMR formula is most accurate for women? ▼

Can I use BMR calculator for weight loss? ▼
