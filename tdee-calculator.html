🎯 What is TDEE?

TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a 24-hour period, including all activities, exercise, and basic bodily functions. Understanding your TDEE is crucial for effective weight management, whether your goal is weight loss, muscle gain, or maintenance.

📊 TDEE Formula (Mifflin-St Jeor + Activity)

  • Men: BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) - (5 × age) + 5
  • Women: BMR = (10 × weight kg) + (6.25 × height cm) - (5 × age) - 161
  • TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor

🏃‍♂️ Activity Levels Explained

  • Sedentary (1.2): Little or no exercise, desk job
  • Lightly Active (1.375): Light exercise 1-3 days/week
  • Moderately Active (1.55): Moderate exercise 3-5 days/week
  • Very Active (1.725): Hard exercise 6-7 days/week
  • Extra Active (1.9): Athlete or physical labor job

🎯 How to Use TDEE for Weight Loss

  • Weight Loss: Eat 300-500 calories less than TDEE
  • Weight Maintenance: Eat equal to TDEE
  • Muscle Gain: Eat 300-500 calories more than TDEE

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

❓ What is TDEE?
TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) is the total number of calories your body burns in a day including all activities, exercise, and basic functions.
❓ How to calculate TDEE?
First calculate your BMR using Mifflin-St Jeor formula, then multiply by your activity level factor (1.2 to 1.9). Our calculator does this automatically.
❓ Is TDEE calculator accurate?
TDEE calculators provide estimates within 10-15% accuracy. For best results, use body fat percentage option which increases accuracy.
❓ What is a good TDEE for weight loss?
For weight loss, eat 300-500 calories less than your TDEE. This creates a calorie deficit of 1-2 pounds per week.
