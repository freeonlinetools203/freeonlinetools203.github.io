Chore Wheel – The Fair Way to Assign Household Tasks

The Chore Wheel is a simple, fun, and impartial tool that randomly selects household chores. Whether you're a parent trying to teach responsibility, a group of roommates splitting duties, or just someone who wants to add surprise to cleaning, this wheel takes the argument out of "who does what."

Our wheel offers three preset categories: Daily Chores (laundry, dishes, vacuuming), Weekly Chores (grocery shopping, bathroom cleaning, window washing), and Monthly Chores (garage organizing, garden work, deep cleaning). You can also add your own custom tasks, change colors, enable dark mode, and even turn on realistic sound effects.

How to Use the Chore Wheel

Step 1: Choose a category – Daily, Weekly, or Monthly – depending on which tasks need assigning.

Step 2: Spin the wheel. Each family member or roommate takes a turn spinning, or you can spin once for the entire group.

Step 3: Whoever the wheel lands on (or the selected chore) does that task. Mark it done, then spin again for the next chore.

For families: Let each child spin and do the chore they land on – it turns responsibility into a game.

For roommates: Assign each chore to a different person by spinning the wheel once per chore.

Why a Chore Wheel Works Better Than a Fixed Schedule

Traditional chore charts can become boring or feel unfair. The chore wheel introduces randomness, which makes the process more exciting. No one can complain that they "always" get the worst task – the wheel decides. It also encourages everyone to learn all the chores, because tomorrow you might land on a different one. Studies show that gamifying household tasks increases participation and reduces friction.

Best Chores to Add for Kids & Teenagers

If you have children, consider adding age‑appropriate tasks:

  • Ages 4-7: "Make bed", "Put toys away", "Feed pet", "Water plants"
  • Ages 8-12: "Empty dishwasher", "Take out trash", "Fold towels", "Sweep floor"
  • Teens: "Clean bathroom", "Vacuum entire house", "Cook one meal", "Mow lawn"

You can easily add these using the edit box.

Roommate Edition – Split Chores Fairly

Living with roommates often leads to disputes over cleaning. The chore wheel removes bias. Each week, spin the wheel for each chore – or assign each roommate a spin and the chore they land on becomes theirs for the week. If someone lands on "Trash", they're responsible. Next week, spin again. Our weekly preset includes tasks like grocery shopping, bathroom scrubbing, and window cleaning – all common friction points.

Customizing Your Chore Wheel

Our tool is fully customizable. You can:

  • Add new chores: Type any chore into the input box and click "Add".
  • Delete chores: Click the ✕ button next to any chore to remove it.
  • Switch between presets: Daily, Weekly, or Monthly – or mix them up.
  • Change wheel colors: Use the built‑in color picker.
  • Enable dark mode: Perfect for evening chore planning.

All changes happen instantly, and you can reset to the original presets with one click.
