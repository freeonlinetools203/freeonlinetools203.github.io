What is a CGPA Calculator?

A cgpa calculator is an academic tool that computes your Cumulative Grade Point Average (CGPA) based on your course grades and credit hours. Understanding how to calculate cgpa is essential for students to track their academic progress, maintain scholarship eligibility, and plan for graduation requirements. Our cgpa calculator out of 4 and cgpa calculator out of 10 modes help students from different educational systems.

How to Calculate CGPA – Step by Step

Learning how to calculate a cgpa involves a simple formula: CGPA = Σ (Grade Points × Credits) ÷ Σ Credits. Each course grade is converted to grade points based on your university's grading scale. For example, if you're using a cgpa calculator uoft (University of Toronto), the scale is 4.0. Our tool handles all these calculations automatically.

GPA to CGPA Calculator – Converting Between Systems

A gpa to cgpa calculator helps students who transfer between different educational systems. Similarly, a cgpa to gpa calculator helps Indian students applying to US universities understand their equivalent GPA.

Target CGPA Calculator – Plan Your Academic Future

A target cgpa calculator helps you determine what grades you need in remaining courses to achieve your desired CGPA. Simply enter your current CGPA, completed credits, desired target CGPA, and remaining credits, and our target cgpa calculator will tell you the required GPA for future semesters.

Supported Grading Systems

  • 🇺🇸 USA Standard (4.0 Scale): A=4.0, A-=3.7, B+=3.3, B=3.0, etc.
  • 🇺🇸 USA Weighted (5.0 Scale): Advanced/AP courses weighted higher.
  • 🇮🇳 India CGPA (10-point): Direct 10-point scale, common in universities.
  • 🇮🇳 India Percentage: Convert percentage to CGPA (divide by 10).
  • 🇬🇧 UK Honours System: First Class, Upper Second, Lower Second, Third.
  • 🇨🇦 Canada (4.33 Scale): A+=4.33, A=4.0, A-=3.67, etc.
  • 🇦🇺 Australia (7.0 Scale): HD=7, D=6, C=5, P=4, F=0.
  • 🇩🇪 Germany (1-5 Scale): Lower is better, converted to standard scale.
  • 🇪🇺 Europe ECTS: A=4.0, B=3.3, C=2.7, D=2.0, E=1.0.
  • 🎓 IB Diploma (1-7): 7=4.0, 6=3.5, 5=3.0, etc.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

What is a CGPA online calculator?
A CGPA online calculator is a web-based tool that helps students compute their Cumulative Grade Point Average by entering course grades and credits. Our tool supports multiple international grading systems.
How to calculate CGPA from GPA?
To convert GPA to CGPA, multiply your GPA by the maximum CGPA scale. For example, a 3.5 GPA on a 4.0 scale equals 8.75 CGPA on a 10-point scale (3.5 × 2.5). Our GPA to CGPA calculator does this automatically.
How to calculate a CGPA for multiple semesters?
To calculate overall CGPA, sum all grade points earned across semesters and divide by total credits completed. Our CGPA calculator handles multiple semesters with the previous record feature.
What is a target CGPA calculator?
A target CGPA calculator helps you determine what grades you need in remaining courses to achieve your desired final CGPA. It's essential for academic planning and scholarship requirements.
What is the difference between CGPA and GPA?
GPA (Grade Point Average) typically refers to a single semester's performance, while CGPA (Cumulative Grade Point Average) is the overall average across all completed semesters. Our cgpa and gpa calculator handles both.
How does a CGPA calculator out of 4 work?
A CGPA calculator out of 4 uses the 4.0 grading scale common in US universities. Letter grades are converted to grade points (A=4.0, B=3.0, etc.), multiplied by credits, then divided by total credits.
What is a CGPA calculator UOFT?
A CGPA calculator UOFT (University of Toronto) follows the 4.0 scale with A+=4.0, A=4.0, A-=3.7, B+=3.3, etc. Our tool includes the UOFT grading scale for accurate calculations.
