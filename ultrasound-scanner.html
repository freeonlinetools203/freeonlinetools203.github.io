What is an Ultrasound Report Scanner?

An Ultrasound Report Scanner is a powerful tool that extracts and analyzes information from pregnancy ultrasound reports. Our tool uses advanced OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology to read text from uploaded images or directly from pasted text. It automatically identifies key pregnancy parameters like Gestational Age (GA), Crown-Rump Length (CRL), Fetal Heart Rate (FHR), and even extracts doctor or clinic phone numbers for instant contact.

Key Parameters Analyzed by Our Tool

1. Gestational Age (GA)

Gestational age indicates how far along the pregnancy is, measured in weeks and days. It's calculated from the first day of the last menstrual period or based on ultrasound measurements like CRL. Normal gestational age progression is crucial for monitoring fetal development.

2. Crown-Rump Length (CRL)

CRL is the measurement from the top of the fetal head (crown) to the bottom of the buttocks (rump). It's most accurate for dating pregnancy between 7-13 weeks. The formula is: Gestational Age (weeks) = CRL (cm) + 6.5.

3. Fetal Heartbeat (FHR)

A normal fetal heart rate ranges from 110-160 beats per minute. The presence of a heartbeat confirms fetal viability. Our tool detects terms like "Present", "Positive", or "Absent" to determine heart health.

4. Amniotic Fluid Index (AFI)

Amniotic fluid protects the fetus and allows for movement. Normal AFI ranges from 5-25 cm. Terms like "Adequate", "Normal", "Low", or "High" indicate fluid levels.
