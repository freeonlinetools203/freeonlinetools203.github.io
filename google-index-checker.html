📊 Complete Guide to Google Index Checker Tool

Last Updated: May 16, 2026 | 15 min read | By SEO Experts

What is Google Index Checker? A Google Index Checker is an SEO tool that helps you determine whether your web pages have been indexed by Google's search database. Indexed pages can appear in search results. Our free tool allows you to check single or multiple URLs quickly.

🎯 What is Google Index Checker?

A Google Index Checker (also called Google Indexing Checker or Indexed Pages Checker) is a tool that verifies if a specific URL is included in Google's search index. When Google crawls and processes a page, it adds it to its index — only then can the page appear in search results. Indexing is a critical first step for SEO.

Our free Google Index Checker tool helps you:

  • Check if your website pages are indexed by Google
  • Bulk check multiple URLs at once
  • Identify which pages are missing from Google's index
  • Monitor indexing progress after submitting sitemaps
  • Verify competitor pages are indexed

⚠️ Why Indexing Matters: If your page isn't indexed, it cannot rank. Approximately 70% of pages on the web are not indexed by Google. Regular indexing checks are essential for SEO success.

📊 How to Check if Website is Indexed by Google

1. Using Google Search Console (Most Accurate)

Google Search Console is the official tool from Google. It shows exactly which pages are indexed, why some pages are not indexed, and provides diagnostic information.

  • Log in to Google Search Console with your website verified.
  • Go to "Pages" section under "Indexing".
  • See all indexed and non-indexed pages with reasons.
  • Use the URL Inspection tool to check individual URLs.

2. Using "site:" Operator in Google Search (Quick Method)

The fastest way to check if a page is indexed is to use Google's "site:" search operator.

  • Type site:example.com/page-url in Google search.
  • If results appear → page is indexed.
  • If "No results found" → page is not indexed.
  • For whole domain: site:example.com shows all indexed pages.

✅ How to Get Your Pages Indexed Faster

  • Submit XML Sitemap: Submit your sitemap in Google Search Console.
  • Use URL Inspection Tool: Request indexing for important pages directly.
  • Build Internal Links: Link new pages from already indexed pages.
  • Get Backlinks: Quality backlinks help Google discover your pages faster.
  • Share on Social Media: Social signals can attract Google crawlers.
  • Update Content Regularly: Fresh content encourages recrawling.
  • Use Google Indexing API: For job postings or video content.

📈 Google Indexing Status Meanings

  • Indexed: Page is in Google's search database and can appear in search results.
  • Not Indexed: Page is not in Google's index. Reasons: new page, blocked by robots.txt, noindex tag, poor quality, or crawl issues.
  • Crawled but not indexed: Google visited but decided not to index due to quality issues.
  • Discovered but not crawled: Google knows about the page but hasn't crawled yet.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

How to check if my website is indexed by Google?
Use the "site:" operator (e.g., site:yourdomain.com) in Google Search, or use Google Search Console's Pages report. Our tool also helps you check individual URLs.
What is Google index checker bulk?
Bulk Google index checker allows you to check multiple URLs at once to see which pages are indexed. Our tool generates site: search links for each URL you enter.
How long does Google take to index a new page?
Typically 4 days to 4 weeks. Submitting to Google Search Console and getting quality backlinks can speed up indexing to 24-48 hours.
What is the difference between crawling and indexing?
Crawling is when Googlebot visits your page. Indexing is when Google adds your page to its search database. A page can be crawled but not indexed.
How to check if a specific page is indexed by Google?
Use "site:example.com/full-url" in Google Search. If the page appears in results, it's indexed. Our tool does this automatically for you.
Why are my pages not indexed?
Common reasons: new website, poor content quality, no backlinks, robots.txt blocking, noindex meta tag, duplicate content, or technical SEO issues.
How to remove a page from Google index?
Add noindex meta tag, block in robots.txt, or use Google Search Console's Removals tool.
