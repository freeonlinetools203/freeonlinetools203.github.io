🎯 What is Scan Report AI?

Scan Report AI is an innovative AI-powered digital tool that extracts and analyzes vital information from pregnancy ultrasound reports. Using advanced OCR (Optical Character Recognition) technology, our tool reads text from uploaded images or directly from pasted text, automatically identifying key pregnancy parameters including Gestational Age (GA), Crown-Rump Length (CRL), Fetal Heartbeat (FHR), Amniotic Fluid Level, and even extracts doctor or clinic phone numbers for instant contact.

Whether you're an expecting mother wanting to understand your ultrasound report better, a healthcare professional needing quick information extraction, or a family member helping someone with pregnancy care, this tool is designed to make ultrasound report analysis simple, fast, and accurate - completely free of charge.

💡 Important Note for Best Results: For optimal OCR accuracy, please ensure your ultrasound report image is:
• Clear and well-lit - Avoid dark or blurry photos
• Straight and aligned - Not tilted or rotated
• High resolution - Text should be readable
• Printed text preferred - Handwritten text may not be recognized accurately

🤰 Key Parameters Analyzed by Our Tool

1. Gestational Age (GA)

Gestational Age indicates how far along the pregnancy is, measured in weeks and days. It is calculated from the first day of the last menstrual period (LMP) or based on ultrasound measurements like CRL. Normal gestational age progression is crucial for monitoring fetal development. Our tool automatically detects phrases like "Gestational Age", "GA", "Pregnancy Weeks", and extracts the numerical value.

Normal Ranges: First trimester (1-13 weeks), Second trimester (14-27 weeks), Third trimester (28-40 weeks).

2. Crown-Rump Length (CRL)

Crown-Rump Length (CRL) is the measurement from the top of the fetal head (crown) to the bottom of the buttocks (rump). It is the most accurate measurement for dating pregnancy between 7-13 weeks. CRL can predict gestational age with an accuracy of ±3-5 days.

3. Fetal Heartbeat (FHR)

The Fetal Heart Rate (FHR) is a critical indicator of fetal health. A normal fetal heart rate ranges from 110 to 160 beats per minute (bpm). The heart can be seen beating on ultrasound as early as 6 weeks of gestation.

4. Amniotic Fluid Index (AFI)

Amniotic fluid protects the fetus, allows for movement, and aids in lung development. Normal AFI ranges from 5-25 cm. Low fluid (oligohydramnios) or high fluid (polyhydramnios) may require medical attention.

5. Fetal Gender

Fetal gender can typically be determined via ultrasound after 18-20 weeks of gestation. The accuracy depends on fetal position and the experience of the sonographer.

👶 Understanding Your Ultrasound Report: A Complete Guide

Ultrasound reports can be confusing for expecting parents. Here's what each term means:

BPD (Biparietal Diameter)

Measurement of the fetal head across the temples. Used to estimate gestational age after 13 weeks.

HC (Head Circumference)

Measurement around the fetal head. Helps assess brain growth and development.

AC (Abdominal Circumference)

Measurement around the fetal belly. Used to estimate fetal weight and nutrition status.

FL (Femur Length)

Measurement of the thigh bone. Indicates overall fetal growth and skeletal development.
